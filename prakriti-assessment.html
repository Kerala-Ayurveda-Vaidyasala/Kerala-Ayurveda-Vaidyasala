Guided Self-Observation

Know Your Prakriti

A calm Ayurvedic self-assessment designed to help visitors explore their likely constitutional pattern through Vata, Pitta, and Kapha. This is not a random internet quiz; it is a structured reflection based on long-term tendencies of body, mind, digestion, climate preference, and behavior.

Before you begin

  • Answer according to your natural long-term tendency, not temporary illness or recent stress.
  • Most people are dual-doshic. The result therefore shows the top two Doshas rather than forcing a single type.
  • This is a guided educational tool. A true classical assessment is always best refined through a direct clinical consultation.

How the result works

Each answer carries a Vata, Pitta, or Kapha score. At the end, the page calculates your dominant pattern and presents:

  • your top two Doshas
  • score bars for all three
  • strengths and tendencies
  • supportive lifestyle orientation

Quick constitutional guide

This small guide helps users understand the language used inside the assessment. The final result below will combine these qualities into a likely constitutional pattern.

Vata

The principle of movement, communication, impulse, variability, sensitivity, and lightness.

  • Dry, cold, light, subtle, mobile, clear
  • Irregular appetite and sleep are common tendencies
  • Quick grasp, quick change, quick imagination

Pitta

The principle of digestion, transformation, precision, sharpness, heat, and vision.

  • Hot, sharp, light, liquid, spreading, slightly oily
  • Strong appetite and intensity are common tendencies
  • Logical, driven, exacting, often highly organized

Kapha

The principle of structure, lubrication, endurance, steadiness, memory, and emotional holding power.

  • Heavy, slow, cool, oily, smooth, dense, stable
  • Slow but steady digestion is common
  • Excellent retention, patience, and long-term endurance
